During the Franco-German War, Napoleon III exiled the monarchy while French forces controlled the country. After the peace treaty and the partition of Belgium, republican forces prevented their return and they fled to the most-developed Dutch colony, that being the Dutch East Indies. Their American colonies (except perhaps Aruba and Curacao) were taken over by the better-developed Republican army.
